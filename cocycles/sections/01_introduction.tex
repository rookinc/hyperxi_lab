\section{Introduction}

Local transport on the flag space of the dodecahedron determines a
rigid global structure combining discrete holonomy and continuous
geometry. The resulting object is a connected $60$-vertex tetravalent
graph $\Gsixty$, canonically identified with the arc-transitive graph
$\mathrm{AT4val}[60,6]$.

This construction admits natural $2$-fold quotients
\[
\Gsixty \to \Gthirty \to \Gfifteen,
\]
where
\[
\Gfifteen \cong L(\mathrm{Petersen}).
\]

The transport system induces a canonical $\mathbb{Z}_2$-valued cocycle
on $\Gfifteen$, called the \emph{Thalean cocycle}, which records the
sheet holonomy of the double cover $\Gthirty \to \Gfifteen$ and defines
a nontrivial class in $H^1(\Gfifteen,\mathbb{Z}_2)$.

From this cocycle one obtains a sector--edge incidence operator $M$
whose algebraic structure is governed by the identity
\[
M M^{\mathsf T} = A^3 + 2A^2 + 2I,
\]
where $A$ is the adjacency matrix of $\Gfifteen$.

This identity induces a canonical polynomial action on the adjacency
algebra of $L(\mathrm{Petersen})$, which we call the \emph{cubic
Thalean representation}. After centering and normalization, the rows
of $M$ yield a spherical embedding of $\Gfifteen$ in $\mathbb{R}^{14}$
with exactly three distinct inner products determined by graph distance.

Thus the transport system decomposes into two complementary structures:
a discrete $\mathbb{Z}_2$-valued cocycle capturing holonomy, and a rigid
spherical geometry arising from sector incidence. Together these encode
both the global obstruction and the metric structure of the Petersen
line-graph core.

While the graph $\mathrm{AT4val}[60,6]$ is known, the transport-based
construction, the Thalean cocycle, and the induced polynomial and
geometric structures appear to be new.
