\section{Introduction}

We construct a $60$-vertex tetravalent graph from transport on the flag
structure of the combinatorial dodecahedron. This graph admits natural
$2$-fold quotients
\[
\Gsixty \to \Gthirty \to \Gfifteen,
\]
where the core graph $\Gfifteen$ is isomorphic to the line graph of the
Petersen graph.

The lifted transport defines a canonical $\ZZtwo$-valued cocycle on
$\Gfifteen$ recording sheet holonomy. This cocycle is nontrivial,
cohomological in nature, and cannot be recovered from local combinatorial
data on the core graph.

The resulting structure exhibits a separation between local symmetry and
global transport invariants, arising naturally from the geometry of the
dodecahedron.
