\section{Introduction}

The dodecahedron carries a rich combinatorial structure arising from its
vertices, edges, faces, flags, and Petrie polygons. In this paper we show
that local transport operations on the dodecahedral flag space give rise
to a highly structured global object: a connected $60$-vertex tetravalent
graph $\Gsixty$, identified as the arc-transitive graph
$\mathrm{AT4val}[60,6]$.

Two natural involutive identifications produce quotient graphs
\[
\Gsixty \to \Gthirty \to \Gfifteen,
\]
where the deepest quotient satisfies
\[
\Gfifteen \cong L(\mathrm{Petersen}).
\]

Beyond this combinatorial identification, the transport system induces a
canonical $\mathbb{Z}_2$-valued cocycle, which we call the
\emph{Thalean cocycle}. This cocycle governs a nontrivial signed $2$-lift
$\Gthirty \to \Gfifteen$ and defines a nontrivial cohomology class.

From this cocycle we construct a sector--edge incidence operator $M$
whose algebraic structure is governed by a polynomial identity in the
adjacency operator,
\[
M M^{\mathsf T} = A^3 + 2A^2 + 2I,
\]
where $A$ is the adjacency matrix of $\Gfifteen$.

This identity defines a canonical polynomial representation of
$L(\mathrm{Petersen})$, which we call the \emph{cubic Thalean representation}.
After centering and normalization, this representation yields a
three-angle spherical configuration in the $14$-dimensional nontrivial
adjacency module.

Thus, a single transport invariant --- the Thalean cocycle --- controls
both the signed covering structure and a canonical geometric realization.

While the graph $\mathrm{AT4val}[60,6]$ is known, the transport-based
construction, the Thalean cocycle, and the resulting cubic Thalean
representation appear to be new.
