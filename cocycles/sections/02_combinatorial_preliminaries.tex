\section{Construction of the Chamber Graph $G_{60}$}

We construct the chamber graph $G_{60}$ from the flag structure of the
combinatorial dodecahedron.

\subsection{The dodecahedron}

Let $D$ be the combinatorial dodecahedron, with
\[
|V(D)| = 20, \qquad |E(D)| = 30, \qquad |F(D)| = 12.
\]

\subsection{Flags}

A \emph{flag} of $D$ is an incident triple
\[
(v,e,f),
\]
where $v$ is a vertex, $e$ an edge incident to $v$, and $f$ a face
incident to $e$.

The dodecahedron has exactly $120$ flags.

\subsection{Local transport on flags}

There are three canonical involutions on flags:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Vertex move} $V$: $(v,e,f) \mapsto (v',e,f)$, where $v'$
is the other endpoint of $e$;

\item \textbf{Edge move} $E$: $(v,e,f) \mapsto (v,e',f)$, where $e'$
is the other edge of $f$ incident to $v$;

\item \textbf{Face move} $F$: $(v,e,f) \mapsto (v,e,f')$, where $f'$
is the other face incident to $e$.
\end{itemize}

Each of $V,E,F$ is an involution. Together they generate the connected
flag graph of $D$.

\subsection{Chamber states}

Each edge $e$ of $D$ is incident to exactly four flags. These four flags
split into two opposite pairs under the subgroup generated by the vertex
and face moves.

A \emph{chamber state} is defined to be one such opposite pair.

Thus the $120$ flags partition into $60$ chamber states, which we take
as the vertex set of $G_{60}$.

\subsection{Induced transport}

The involutions $V,E,F$ on flags descend to well-defined operations on
chamber states. Because opposite flags are identified, certain flag moves
coincide after passing to chamber states.

Each chamber state admits exactly four distinct neighbors under the
induced transport, yielding a $4$-regular structure.

\subsection{The chamber graph}

The graph $G_{60}$ is defined on the set of chamber states, with two
states adjacent if one is obtained from the other by an induced transport
move.

\begin{proposition}
The graph $G_{60}$ is connected and $4$-regular with $60$ vertices.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Each chamber state admits exactly four distinct induced transport moves,
giving degree $4$. Connectivity follows from the connectedness of the
flag graph, since the quotient identification respects the transport
structure. A computational verification is given in Section~13.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Quotient structure}

The transport construction admits natural $2$-fold quotients
\[
G_{60} \longrightarrow G_{30} \longrightarrow G_{15}.
\]

The graph $G_{15}$ has $15$ vertices and $30$ edges and will be
identified with the line graph of the Petersen graph in the next
section.

\subsection{Notation}

Throughout the paper:
\begin{itemize}
\item $G_{60}, G_{30}, G_{15}$ denote the graphs defined above,
\item edges are written as unordered pairs $(u,v)$,
\item all graphs are simple and undirected.
\end{itemize}
