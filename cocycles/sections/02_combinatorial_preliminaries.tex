\section{Combinatorial Preliminaries}

We recall basic facts about the combinatorial dodecahedron and its flag
structure.

The dodecahedron has:
\[
20 \text{ vertices}, \quad 30 \text{ edges}, \quad 12 \text{ faces}.
\]

A flag consists of a mutually incident triple
\[
(v,e,f).
\]

There are $120$ flags in total. Opposite flags along an edge define
equivalence classes of size $2$, yielding $60$ chamber states.

Transport operations on flags induce adjacency relations between chamber
states, forming the graph $\Gsixty$.
