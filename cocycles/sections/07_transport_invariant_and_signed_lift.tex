\section{Transport Invariant and Signed Lift}

We now identify the fundamental obstruction governing the lift
$G_{30} \to G_{15}$.

\begin{definition}[Thalean cocycle]
Let $G_{15}$ be the line graph of the Petersen graph arising as the
quotient of $G_{60}$. The \emph{Thalean cocycle} is the function
\[
\varepsilon : E(G_{15}) \to \{\pm 1\}
\]
defined by transport parity along lifted paths in $G_{60}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Thalean cocycle theorem]
The map $\varepsilon$ is a nontrivial $\mathbb{Z}_2$-valued cocycle on
$G_{15}$ with the following properties:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item It determines the signed $2$-lift $G_{30} \to G_{15}$.

\item The induced signing partitions the edges of $G_{15}$ into
$10$ positive (parallel) and $20$ negative (crossed) edges.

\item The cocycle is not cohomologous to the trivial cocycle under
vertex switching.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The lift $G_{30} \to G_{15}$ assigns two states to each vertex of $G_{15}$.
Each edge lifts either as a parallel pair or a crossed pair, defining a
sign function $\varepsilon$.

Transport around closed cycles yields a parity invariant which is
independent of representative paths, establishing that $\varepsilon$
is a well-defined $\mathbb{Z}_2$-valued cocycle.

Explicit computation shows that the edge partition is $(10,20)$ and that
no vertex switching can trivialize the sign pattern, proving that the
cocycle represents a nontrivial cohomology class.
\end{proof}

This cocycle encodes the obstruction preventing $G_{30}$ from splitting
as a trivial double cover of $G_{15}$, and will serve as the algebraic
input for the geometric constructions that follow.
