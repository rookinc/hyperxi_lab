\section{Sector Incidence Algebra}

Let $G \cong L(\mathrm{Petersen})$ be the $15$-vertex line graph of the
Petersen graph, with adjacency matrix $A$. The graph $G$ is
distance-regular, and its spectral and algebraic properties are well
documented (see \cite{brouwer_cohen_neumaier,godsil_royle}).

\subsection{Sectors and the sector--edge incidence matrix}

We now make precise the incidence object underlying the transport system.

\begin{definition}[Transport sector]
For each vertex $v \in V(G)$, define the \emph{transport sector}
$S(v) \subseteq E(G)$ to be the set of edges of $G$ arising from the
transport construction when rooted at $v$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Sector--edge incidence matrix]
Let $V(G)=\{v_1,\dots,v_{15}\}$ and $E(G)=\{e_1,\dots,e_{30}\}$. The
\emph{sector--edge incidence matrix}
\[
M=(M_{ij}) \in \mathbb{R}^{15 \times 30}
\]
is defined by
\[
M_{ij} =
\begin{cases}
1 & \text{if } e_j \in S(v_i),\\
0 & \text{if } e_j \notin S(v_i).
\end{cases}
\]
Equivalently, the $i$-th row of $M$ is the indicator vector of the sector
$S(v_i)$.
\end{definition}

One verifies that each sector has size $14$, and each edge lies in exactly
$7$ sectors:
\[
\sum_e M_{v,e} = 14, \qquad
\sum_v M_{v,e} = 7.
\]

\subsection{The sector identity}

\begin{theorem}[Sector identity]
Let $A$ be the adjacency matrix of $G$. Then
\[
M M^{\mathsf T} = A^3 + 2A^2 + 2I.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let $p(x)=x^3+2x^2+2$. Since $G$ has spectrum
\[
\{-2^{(5)},\, -1^{(4)},\, 2^{(5)},\, 4^{(1)}\},
\]
the operator $p(A)$ has eigenvalues
\[
\{2^{(5)},\, 3^{(4)},\, 18^{(5)},\, 98^{(1)}\}.
\]

Direct computation shows that $M M^{\mathsf T}$ has the same spectrum.
Since both operators lie in the adjacency algebra of $G$, they coincide.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Algebraic interpretation}

The operator $M$ defines a canonical element of the Bose--Mesner algebra
of $G$, with
\[
M M^{\mathsf T} = p(A).
\]

Thus the transport system induces a polynomial representation of the
adjacency algebra.

\subsection{The cubic Thalean representation}

\begin{definition}
The map
\[
A \;\mapsto\; A^3 + 2A^2 + 2I
\]
is called the \emph{cubic Thalean representation}.
\end{definition}

If $Av=\lambda v$, then
\[
M M^{\mathsf T}v = p(\lambda)v.
\]
Thus the representation acts by spectral transformation via $p(x)$.

\subsection{Quadratic blindness to the cocycle}

Although the sector system and the transport cocycle arise from the same
underlying construction, the quadratic sector observables do not detect
the cocycle.

Indeed, the operator $M M^{\mathsf T}$ depends only on the overlap
pattern of sector supports and is therefore insensitive to the
$\ZZtwo$-valued holonomy carried by the lift. In this sense, the sector
identity records the global incidence geometry of transport, while the
cocycle remains a loop-level invariant detected only through holonomy.

Accordingly, the polynomial identity above should be understood as a
statement about the quadratic geometry of the transport sectors, not as
a spectral detection of the cocycle itself.
