\section{Spherical Sector Geometry}

\subsection{Centering and Normalization}

Let $\mathbf{1}$ denote the all-ones vector and define
\[
M_c = M - \frac{14}{30}\,\mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}^T.
\]

Then the rows of $M_c$ lie in the $14$-dimensional subspace orthogonal to
$\mathbf{1}$. Normalize:
\[
\widetilde{M}_v = \frac{(M_c)_v}{\|(M_c)_v\|}.
\]

\subsection{Three-Angle Structure}

\begin{theorem}
The vectors $\{\widetilde{M}_v\}$ form a spherical configuration in
$\mathbb{R}^{14}$ with exactly three distinct inner products:
\[
\langle \widetilde{M}_u, \widetilde{M}_v \rangle \in \{\alpha,\beta,\gamma\},
\quad u \neq v,
\]
where numerically
\[
\alpha \approx -0.339286,\quad
\beta \approx -0.205357,\quad
\gamma \approx 0.330357.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The Gram matrix is obtained from $M M^{\mathsf T}$ after centering and
normalization. Since $M M^{\mathsf T} = p(A)$ lies in the adjacency
algebra and $G$ has diameter $3$, inner products depend only on graph
distance. Direct computation yields exactly three values.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Geometric Interpretation}

The embedding $v \mapsto \widetilde{M}_v$ realizes $G$ as a spherical
three-angle configuration in $\mathbb{R}^{14}$.

The three inner products correspond exactly to graph distances
$1,2,3$, showing that the geometry is completely determined by the
adjacency structure.

\subsection{Geometric Realization of the Representation}

This embedding is the geometric realization of the cubic Thalean
representation. The polynomial identity
\[
M M^{\mathsf T} = p(A)
\]
translates directly into metric relations in the embedding, making the
geometry canonical.
