\section{Discussion}

The construction produces two global structures:

\begin{itemize}
\item the \emph{Thalean cocycle}, encoding a nontrivial
$\mathbb{Z}_2$-obstruction to local transport;
\item the \emph{Thalean sector system}, encoding a rigid overlap
structure governed by a polynomial in the adjacency operator.
\end{itemize}

Together, these show that local transport on the dodecahedral flag
space generates both cohomological and geometric structure on the
Petersen line-graph core.

This dual emergence suggests that transport systems on combinatorial
geometries may systematically produce algebraic invariants and
highly constrained spherical configurations.
