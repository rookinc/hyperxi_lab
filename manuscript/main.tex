\begin{abstract}
Local transport on the flag space of the combinatorial dodecahedron
produces a connected $60$-vertex tetravalent graph $G_{60}$, canonically
identified with the arc-transitive graph $\mathrm{AT4val}[60,6]$
(\cite{potocnik_graphsym,house_of_graphs,potocnik_private}).

This construction admits successive $2$-fold quotients
\[
G_{60} \to G_{30} \to G_{15},
\]
where $G_{15} \cong L(\mathrm{Petersen})$, a classical object in algebraic
graph theory (\cite{godsil_royle}).

The lifted transport induces a canonical $\mathbb{Z}_2$-valued cocycle on
$G_{15}$, called the \emph{Thalean cocycle}, which governs the signed
$2$-lift $G_{30} \to G_{15}$ and represents a nontrivial class in
$H^1(G_{15},\mathbb{Z}_2)$. This cocycle captures the holonomy of transport
and cannot be recovered from local combinatorial data.

From the same transport system we construct a sector--edge incidence
operator $M \in \mathbb{R}^{15 \times 30}$ satisfying
\[
M M^{\mathsf T} = A^3 + 2A^2 + 2I,
\]
where $A$ is the adjacency matrix of $G_{15}$.
After centering and normalization, this yields a spherical embedding of
$G_{15}$ in $\mathbb{R}^{14}$ with exactly three distinct inner products.

Thus a single combinatorial transport mechanism simultaneously produces
a nontrivial cohomological invariant and a rigid geometric realization on
the Petersen line-graph core, linking discrete holonomy, adjacency
algebras, and spherical configurations in a unified framework.
\end{abstract}
