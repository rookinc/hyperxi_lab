\begin{abstract}

We study a transport system on the flag space of the dodecahedron
generated by Petrie traversal.  Collapsing the orientation redundancy
of the $120$ flags yields a $60$-state chamber system whose adjacency
structure forms a $4$-regular graph.

We call this graph the Thalean graph and its vertices thalions.
We analyze its combinatorial, spectral, and symmetry properties and
show that it coincides with the arc-transitive tetravalent graph
$\mathrm{AT4val}[60,6]$.

\end{abstract}
