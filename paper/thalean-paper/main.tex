\begin{abstract}

We construct a $60$-vertex tetravalent graph from local transport
operations on the flag structure of the combinatorial dodecahedron.
Starting from the $120$ flags of the polyhedron, we identify opposite
configurations along edges to obtain $60$ chamber states. Two transport
moves derived from vertex and face operations induce adjacency
relations between these states, producing a connected $4$-regular graph
$G_{60}$. The graph contains $40$ triangles, has diameter $6$, and has
shell profile $(1,4,8,16,24,6,1)$.

The graph admits a free action of the Klein four group
$V_4 \cong \mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_2$. The quotient graph
$Q_{15}=G_{60}/V_4$ has $15$ vertices and is naturally identified with
the graph of opposite-edge classes of the dodecahedron. This graph is
isomorphic to the line graph of the Petersen graph.

Consequently $G_{60}$ is a regular $V_4$-cover of
$L(\mathrm{Petersen})$. The graph coincides with the arc-transitive
graph $\mathrm{AT4val}[60,6]$ in the census of tetravalent graphs.
The construction provides a direct geometric realization of this graph
arising from transport on the dodecahedral flag complex.

\end{abstract}
