\section{Chamber States}

We reduce the flag set of the dodecahedron to obtain the vertices of
our graph.

\begin{definition}

Two flags $(v,e,f)$ and $(v',e,f')$ are called \emph{opposite} if
$v,v'$ are the endpoints of $e$ and $f,f'$ are the two faces incident
to $e$.

The unordered pair
\[
[(v,e,f)] = \{(v,e,f),(v',e,f')\}
\]
is called a \emph{chamber state}.

\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}
The number of chamber states is $60$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}

Each edge has four associated flags.

The definition pairs these flags into two opposite pairs.

Since the dodecahedron has $30$ edges, we obtain

\[
2 \times 30 = 60
\]

chamber states.

\end{proof}

These chamber states form the vertex set of the graph constructed in
the next section.
