\section{Combinatorics of the Dodecahedron}
\label{sec:dodec}

Let $D$ denote the combinatorial dodecahedron.

The dodecahedron has
\[
|V(D)| = 20, \qquad |E(D)| = 30, \qquad |F(D)| = 12.
\]

\subsection{Flags}

A \emph{flag} is an incident triple
\[
(v,e,f)
\]
such that
\[
v \in e \subset f.
\]

Since every edge has two endpoints and two incident faces, the number
of flags is
\[
|\mathcal{F}(D)| = 4|E(D)| = 120.
\]

\subsection{Flag Operations}

Let $(v,e,f)$ be a flag.

\begin{definition}

\textbf{Vertex move $V$.}

Let $v'$ be the other endpoint of $e$.

\[
V(v,e,f) = (v',e,f).
\]

\textbf{Face move $F$.}

Let $f'$ be the other face incident to $e$.

\[
F(v,e,f) = (v,e,f').
\]

\end{definition}

These operations generate the transport moves used to construct the
graph $G_{60}$.
