\section{Basic Graph Properties}

We now record several structural properties of the graph $G_{60}$.

\begin{proposition}
The graph $G_{60}$ has
\[
|V(G_{60})| = 60, \qquad |E(G_{60})| = 120 .
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The vertex set is the set $C(D)$ of chamber states, which has size
$60$ by Proposition~3.1.  Since $G_{60}$ is $4$-regular, the number of
edges is
\[
|E(G_{60})| = \frac{1}{2}\cdot 60\cdot 4 = 120 .
\]
\end{proof}

Computational analysis of the graph reveals the following invariants.

\begin{proposition}
The graph $G_{60}$ contains $40$ triangles and has diameter $6$.
\end{proposition}

The metric structure of the graph may be described by its shell
profile.

\begin{proposition}
The shell profile of $G_{60}$ is
\[
(1,4,8,16,24,6,1).
\]
\end{proposition}

These parameters indicate that $G_{60}$ has a highly structured
combinatorial organization rather than the behavior typical of a
random tetravalent graph.

Further structural insight will arise from the symmetries of the graph
described in the next section.
