\section{The Opposite-Edge Graph of the Dodecahedron}

Two edges of the dodecahedron are called \emph{opposite} if they lie on
a common pentagonal face and are separated by exactly one edge along
that face.

Considering this relation across all faces partitions the $30$ edges
of the dodecahedron into $15$ opposite-edge classes.

\begin{definition}
Let $K_D$ denote the graph whose vertices correspond to these
$15$ opposite-edge classes.

Two vertices of $K_D$ are adjacent whenever the corresponding edge
pairs share a common endpoint of the dodecahedron.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}
The graph $K_D$ has $15$ vertices and is $4$-regular.
\end{proposition}

We will show in the next section that this graph coincides with the
quotient graph $Q_{15}$.
