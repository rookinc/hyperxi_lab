\section{Identification with the Petersen Line Graph}

We now identify the quotient graph $Q_{15}$ with the opposite-edge
graph $K_D$ introduced in the previous section.

\begin{theorem}
\[
Q_{15} \cong K_D .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Each orbit of the $V_4$-action corresponds naturally to an
opposite-edge class of the dodecahedron.

Adjacency between two orbits occurs precisely when the corresponding
edge pairs share a vertex of the dodecahedron.  This matches exactly
the adjacency relation defining the graph $K_D$.
\end{proof}

It is a classical observation (see, for example,
\cite{godsil_royle}) that the opposite-edge graph of the
dodecahedron is isomorphic to the line graph of the Petersen graph.

\begin{proposition}
\[
K_D \cong L(\mathrm{Petersen}) .
\]
\end{proposition}

Combining these identifications yields the main structural
description of the quotient graph.
