\section{$V_4$ Symmetry}

A central feature of the graph $G_{60}$ is the presence of two
commuting involutions acting on its vertex set.

\begin{proposition}
There exist two involutions $a$ and $b$ acting on $V(G_{60})$ which
commute and act freely on the vertex set.
\end{proposition}

These involutions arise from symmetries of the dodecahedral transport
structure and exchange chamber states in pairs.

\begin{proposition}
The group generated by $a$ and $b$ is isomorphic to the Klein four
group
\[
\langle a,b\rangle \cong \mathbb{Z}_2^2 .
\]
\end{proposition}

Since the action is free, the vertex set of $G_{60}$ decomposes into
orbits of size $4$.

\begin{corollary}
The action partitions the vertex set of $G_{60}$ into $15$ orbits.
\end{corollary}

These orbits will serve as the vertices of the quotient graph
constructed in the next section.
